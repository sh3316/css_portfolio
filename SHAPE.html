Project: SHAPE – Automated Manufacturing of Patient-Specific Fracture Plates

The SHAPE project aims to revolutionize fracture treatment by integrating additive manufacturing (AM), computer modeling, and programming technologies. Current treatments rely on mass-produced flat plates, which often cause stress localization and poor healing due to their lack of anatomical compatibility. SHAPE addresses these limitations by creating semi-helical, patient-specific plates that conform precisely to the curvature of individual bones.

Using Python, CT scans are converted into accurate 3D CAD models, which are then processed into personalized plate designs. The helical geometry distributes stress more evenly, reducing complications such as malunion and refractures. This innovative approach eliminates the need for manual plate bending during surgery, saving time and minimizing risks of surgical site infections (SSI).

The project also explored the optimal configuration of screw placement and insertion angles. Based on research, a default screw angle of perpendicular to the bone surface was implemented to reduce stress, while adjustments allow for variations based on fracture location. The final design uses five screws to balance structural stability and infection prevention. The resulting plates demonstrated superior mechanical properties during simulation tests, with stress concentrations well below critical thresholds.

Despite the higher cost and longer production time compared to conventional plates, SHAPE presents a compelling case for improving recovery rates and surgical efficiency. Advances in automation and additive manufacturing are expected to lower costs, making this innovative approach increasingly viable in clinical settings.

Topics: Additive Manufacturing, Biomechanical Engineering, Computational Modeling, Custom Medical Devices, Surgical Optimization

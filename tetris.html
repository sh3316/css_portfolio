Project: Tiling Algorithms for Randomly Generated Surfaces

Developed an advanced algorithm to tile randomly generated surfaces with Tetris pieces (tetrominoes), achieving approximately 90% accuracy and maintaining efficient runtimes. The algorithm employed a greedy strategy, prioritizing tiles with the most free edges and dynamically updating free edge counts to guide the placement of pieces. This approach mimicked human problem-solving by starting with the outer areas or tiles with fewer neighbors, ensuring effective coverage.

When multiple tetrominoes fit a location, the algorithm selected the piece maximizing edge coverage, reducing gaps in the solution. Further enhancements included detecting ‘tromino’ zones (three-square areas) and prioritizing their coverage with tetrominoes to minimize exposed tiles, mitigating common challenges of greedy methods. The solution efficiently handled target surfaces on grids of up to 1000 x 1000, demonstrating robust performance for various complex configurations.

Topics: Algorithm Design, Greedy Methods, Computational Geometry, Optimization Strategies, Problem Solving
